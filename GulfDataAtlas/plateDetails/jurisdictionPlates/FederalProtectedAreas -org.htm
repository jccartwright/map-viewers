• Protected Areas - Federal

    Federal protected areas in the United States are lands and waters owned and/or managed by the federal government to preserve or enhance natural, historical, cultural, wildlife, and aesthetic values and resources.

    National Estuarine Research System
    The National Estuarine Research Reserve System in the Gulf of Mexico consists of five protected areas established by partnerships between the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) and coastal states. The National Estuarine Research Reserve System protects more than 460,000 acres of coastal and estuarine habitats for long-term research, water-quality monitoring, education, and coastal stewardship in the Gulf of Mexico alone.

    National Forests
    The National Forests are largely forest and woodland areas owned by the federal government and managed by the United States Forest Service, part of the United States Department of Agriculture. Land management of these areas focuses on timber harvesting, livestock grazing, water, wildlife, and recreation. Over 8 million acres of land are designated as National Forests in Alabama, Florida, Louisiana, Mississippi, and Texas.

    National Marine Sanctuaries
    The National Marine Sanctuaries are federally designated areas within the United States waters that protect areas of the marine environment with special conservation, recreational, ecological, historical, cultural, archeological, scientific, educational, or aesthetic qualities. Within the Gulf of Mexico there are two national marine sanctuaries that encompass more than 245,000 acres. The National Marine Sanctuaries Program (NMSP), a division of the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA), administers to all the marine sanctuaries.

    National Park System
    The National Park System is run by the National Park Service, a bureau of the Department of the Interior. The system is made up of monuments, memorials, parks, preserves, and seashores. Over 4 million acres have been designated as a part of the National Park System for the states of Alabama, Florida, Louisiana, Mississippi, and Texas.

    National Wildlife Refuge System
    The National Wildlife Refuge System is the system of public lands and waters set aside to conserve America's fish, wildlife and plants. They are protected areas of the United States managed by the United States Fish and Wildlife Service. The mission of the Refuge System is to manage a national network of lands and waters for the conservation, management, and where appropriate, restoration of fish, wildlife and plant resources and their habitat. Almost 6 million acres have been designated as National Wildlife Refuges in the Gulf of Mexico region.
