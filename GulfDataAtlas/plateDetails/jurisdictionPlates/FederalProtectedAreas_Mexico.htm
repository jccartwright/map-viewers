• Federal Protected Areas
    Mexico

    Protected Areas in Mexico are managed by the National Commission of Protected Natural Areas (Comision Nacional de Areas Naturales Protegidas [CONANP]), whose mission is to "conserve the national heritage of Mexico through Protected Areas and other forms of preservation by promoting a culture of conservation and the sustainable development of communities living in their own environment". There are currently over 25 million hectares, or 12.92% of national territory under environmental management within nine CONANP regions. Management categories of the Federal Natural Protected Areas in Mexico are defined by the General Act of Ecological Equilibrium and Environmental Protection (Ley General de Equilibrio Ecologico y Proteccion al Ambiente [LGEEPA]) (CONANP, 2014).

    Biosphere Reserves
    Biosphere Reserves are nationally-representative biogeographic areas encompassing one or more ecosystems that are not significantly altered by human-related impacts and do not require preservation and/or restoration actions. These areas are inhabited by key species representative of national biodiversity, including endemic species that are threatened or endangered. Access is limited to scientific research, environmental education, and conservation activities (CONANP, 2014).

    National Parks
    National Parks are nationally-representative biogeographic areas encompassing one or more ecosystems that are selected for their scenic beauty, scientific, educational, recreational or historical value, the presence of distinctive flora and fauna and their potential for tourism. National Parks are managed for the protection of natural resources, flora and fauna, and the general preservation of ecosystems, as well as for scientific research, tourism, and environmental education (CONANP, 2014).

    National Monuments
    Natural Monuments are established to protect sites of one or more natural features, either places or natural objects that exhibit unique or exceptional aesthetic, scientific, or historical value. These areas are not required to have the ecological diversity of other protected area categories. Access is restricted to preservation, scientific research, recreation, and education activities (CONANP, 2014).

    Natural Resources Protected Areas
    These areas are established to preserve and protect the soil, watersheds, water, and other natural resources such as rivers, lakes, ponds, and streams within forest areas that are not within any other managed site, to ensure protection, preservation and sustainable use. These resources often supply for human communities and are considered national waters.

    Flora and Fauna Protection Areas
    These are areas where the ecological balance and preservation of habitats depend on the existence and evolutionary development of wild flora and fauna species. These areas are established under provisions of the Ecological Act, Wildlife Act and the Fisheries Act. Permitted activities include the preservation, repopulation propagation, acclimatization, refuge, research, and sustainable use of species (CONANP, 2014).

    Wildlife Sanctuaries
    These Sanctuaries are established to protect areas characterized by a considerable wealth of flora or fauna or by the presence of species or subspecies that have a restricted biogeographic distribution. These areas can encompass glens, vegas, caves, caverns, sinkholes, creeks or other topographic or geographic units. Research, recreation, and educational activities consistent with the nature and characteristics or the area are permitted (CONANP, 2014).
