• Watersheds

    A watershed is an area of land in which water drains to a point where it combines with another, usually larger, water body. The draining water may be surface water (i.e., a stream) or rain and melted snow runoff. The water typically drains into a river, lake, wetland, or ocean. Some watersheds drain into an arid region where the water evaporates away. This graphic shows the watersheds that drain into the Gulf of Mexico.

    The United States Geological Survey has classified the United States into four levels: regions, subregions, accounting units, and cataloging units. Each hydrologic unit is identified by a unique numeric code (HUC) consisting of two to eight digits based upon the four levels of classification. Using these associated codes provides a standardized base for locating, storing, retrieving, and exchanging hydrologic data.

    Region:    This first level of classication divides the United States into 21 major geographic regions. A region contains either the drainage area of a major river or the combined drainage areas of a series of rivers.
    Subregion:    This second level of classification divides the 21 regions into 221 subregions. A subregion includes the area drained by a river system or a group of streams forming a coastal drainage area.
    Accounting Unit:    This third level of classification subdivides many of the subregions into 378 accounting units.
    Cataloging Unit:    This fourth level of classification is the smallest element of a hydrologic unit. The United States is composed of 2,264 cataloging units that represent part or all of a surface drainage basin, a combination of drainage basins, or a distinct hydrologic feature. These cataloging units sometimes are called "watersheds".

    The United States Geological Survey has made recent improvements to the HUC efforts by adding a fifth and sixth level. This new dataset is called the Watershed Boundary Dataset (WBD) and it defines hydrologic units down to 8-digit, 10-digit, and 12-digit HUCs, which are shown in this map along with the Mexican basin and subbasin watersheds.
