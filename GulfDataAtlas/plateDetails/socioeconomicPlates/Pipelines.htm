• Oil and Gas Pipelines

    If placed end to end, the oil and gas pipelines in the Gulf of Mexico would wrap around the Earth's equator. The Gulf of Mexico region's oil and gas industry, one of the most developed in the world, supplies the region with jobs and the Nation with a valuable energy source. Nearly all current offshore leasing (a commercial arrangement where the lessee will select/purchase an area offshore from the state or federal government to extract natural resources) and development activity occurs in the central and western Gulf of Mexico. There are over 26,000 miles of oil and gas pipeline on the Gulf of Mexico seafloor. (NOAA's State of the Coast - Energy)

    Some oil and gas pipelines contain high concentrations of hydrogen sulfide (H2S), and these are identified on the map. Hydrogen sulfide gas is a highly toxic contaminant and a safety hazard, and as a result these pipelines are monitored, equipped with alarm systems, and subject to various other safety measures and protocols.
