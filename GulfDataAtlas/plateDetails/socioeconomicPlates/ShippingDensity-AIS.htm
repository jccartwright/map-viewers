    Vessel Density - 2010

    This map showing Commercial Vessel Density from October 2009 to October 2010 was developed by NOAA's Office of Coast Survey (OCS). It is based on data from the U.S. Coast Guard's Automatic Identification System (AIS). The AIS tracks marine vessels and records information such as position, speed, heading, vessel identification, and vessel type.

    Commercial-use vessels were the focus of this map, thus shipping fairways and port areas show the densest activity. The patterns revealed could be used for planning purposes. For example, a proposed area for a marine sanctuary may coincide with a heavily traveled shipping corridor. In that case, a different proposed area may be chosen for the sanctuary to avoid conflicts with the commercial industry.

    One interesting part of the mapped pattern is the orange circle to the southeast of New Orleans, LA. That is the location of the April 2010 Deepwater Horizon oil spill, which resulted in much cleanup and scientific activity in the area during the summer and fall of 2010.

    Vessel Density - 2011

    The 2011 vessel density shows a pattern that is similar to the 2010 pattern. The 2011 dataset is less detailed, showing a range of high to low density rather than actual numbers. From the overall dataset, specific vessel types have been broken out into individual maps: cargo vessels, fishing vessels, passenger vessels, pleasure craft and sailing vessels, tankers, and tug and towing vessels. Not all vessels in the Gulf of Mexico use the AIS system, so density is estimated based on vessels that do participate.
