    U.S. and Mexican Ports
    Gulf of Mexico

    Trade statistics for coastal ports are important indicators of local and national coastal economies, contributing to analyses of community and industry interactions and natural resource use. This map depicts the locations of U.S. and Mexican ports in the Gulf of Mexico. Graphs showing annual summaries of foreign, domestic, and total trade by weight from 2005 to 2012 for the top 15 ports can be viewed by clicking on the links below. The accompanying tables show the summarized statistics for all the Gulf of Mexico ports.

    These data have been combined into a single Esri shapefile for the Gulf of Mexico that gives the locations and trade statistics for each port.

    Waterborne commerce statistics for U.S. principle ports are compiled and distributed by the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers (USACE) Waterborne Commerce Statistics Center (WCSC), Institute for Water Resources (IWR). Mexican data are provided by the Directorate General of Ports in statistical yearbooks of port operations.
