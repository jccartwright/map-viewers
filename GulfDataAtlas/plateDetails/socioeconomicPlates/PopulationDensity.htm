• Population Density - United States

    Population density is the number of people per square mile. This map illustrates the population density along the U.S. coastline in 2010.

    Population density is calculated by dividing the total population count of a geographic feature by the area of the feature, in square miles. The area is calculated from the geometry of the geographic feature in projected coordinates. For this map, the initial geographic features are the county boundaries of each state, switching to Census tract boundaries when the map is at its highest resolution.

    The source of the data is Esri's Updated Demographics (2010/2015).
