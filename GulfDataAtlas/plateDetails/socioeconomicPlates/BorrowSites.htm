• Borrow Sites

    Coastal restoration, beach nourishment, and levee reconstruction are crucial to mitigate future coastal erosion, land loss, flooding, and storm damage in the Gulf of Mexico. The success of that long-term effort depends on locating and securing significant quantities of outer continental shelf sediment resources that are compatible with and can act as borrow sites for the target environments being restored. Offshore sand resources, like upland sources, are extremely scarce where most needed. Additionally, vast areas of these relatively small offshore sand resources are not extractable because of the presence of oil and gas infrastructure, as well as archaeologically and biologically sensitive areas.

    Outer continental shelf sediment resources refer to the sediment deposit(s), including clay, silt, sand, and gravel-sized particles and shell, found on or below the surface of the seabed on the outer continental shelf, as defined in Section 2(s) of the Outer Continental Shelf Lands Act (43 U.S.C 1331(a)).
