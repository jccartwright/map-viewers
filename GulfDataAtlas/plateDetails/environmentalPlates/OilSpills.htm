    Oil Spills (2000 - 2010)

    Oil spills stem from accidents involving tankers, barges, pipelines, refineries, and storage facilities, often while the oil is being transported to its users. Some of the major oil spills (> 100,000 gallons of oil released) to occur in the Gulf of Mexico from 2000 to 2010 would include the 2005 Hurricane Katrina damages to facilities and tanks in Plaquemines Parish, Saint Bernard Parish, and Mississippi River (over 10,000,000 gallons); the 2006 CITGO Refinery waste oil tank release (42,000 - 1,470,000 gallons); the 2008 Barge DM932 collision (380,000 gallons); and the 2010 Deepwater Horizon semisubmersible drilling platform explosion (207,060,000 gallons). A time series of oil spill incidents from 2000 to 2010 is available.

    The IncidentNews website provides publicly available information related to oil and hazardous material spills, both current and historical. It is developed and maintained by NOAA's Emergency Response Division (ERD), which is part of NOAA's Office of Response and Restoration (OR&R). ERD was formerly known as the Hazardous Materials Response Division, or HAZMAT. The volume of the Deepwater Horizon release was taken from Assessment of Flow Rate Estimates for the Deepwater Horizon/Macondo Oil Spill published by the National Incident Command, Interagency Solutions Group, Flow Rate Technical Group.
