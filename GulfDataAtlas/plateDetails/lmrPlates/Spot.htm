• Spot
    Leiostomus xanthurus

    Spot (Leiostomus xanthurus), or spot croaker, are distributed throughout the entire Gulf of Mexico, but are most abundant in the north-central Gulf region. Spot tend to remain in the shallow coastal waters throughout the winter (typically <60 m), and then move inshore into bays and sounds with the onset of the warmer temperatures. Adults favor sand or mud bottom. Spawning takes place offshore in the immediate vicinity of tidal passes during the winter months between December and March. Eggs and larvae are transported into the upper reaches of the estuarine system.

    This species is commonly up to 10 inches long (total length), but can grow to a maximum of 14 inches. Spot feed primarily on bottom-dwelling worms and crustaceans. (Texas Parks & Wildlife Department, South Carolina Department of Natural Resources)  

    This map shows the fisheries-independent (federal and state) trawl stations used to calculate catch per unit effort (CPUE) data for spot.

    Fisheries-Independent Data
    Federal

    Relative abundance and sampling effort for spot in 10-minute longitude by latitude blocks are summarized from 11,637 40-ft trawls taken during the SEAMAP Summer and Fall Shrimp/Groundfish surveys from 1987 to 2009. Relative abundance is expressed in catch per unit effort (CPUE) which represents the number caught per 1-hour tow. Sampling effort represents the number of trawls. These fish are primarily adult but may represent some juveniles as well.

    Fisheries-Independent Data
    State
    Using data from state sources, maps displaying spot mean catch per unit effort (CPUE) based on individual sampling locations, as well as by water bodies are available. The information shows the summarized results from 4,287 trawls (from primarily 16-ft to 20-ft otter trawls) taken from 2000 to 2007 in estuarine and nearshore habitats. This trawl data set was obtained from the Comparative Assessment of Gulf Estuarine ecoSystems (CAGES) Relational Database, which is a compilation of estuarine fishery-independent data contributed by natural resource agencies of the Gulf States (Texas, Louisiana, Mississippi, Alabama, and Florida). There are some differences in each state's gear and sampling methodologies, and CPUE values between states may not be directly comparable. CPUE is expressed in number caught per hectare.
