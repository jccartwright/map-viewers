• Atlantic Croaker
    Micropogonias undulatus

    The Atlantic croaker (Micropogonias undulatus) is found throughout the Gulf of Mexico in estuaries and offshore waters, typically on sand and mud bottoms inshore and on a wide variety of bottom types offshore. Larger Atlantic croaker show some attraction to offshore oil and gas platforms in the northern Gulf of Mexico.

    The distribution of Atlantic croaker in the northern Gulf is dependent on water temperature, season, and bottom type, but they are generally found in depths less than 110 m except off the mouth of the Mississippi River. During late spring, summer, and early fall, when inshore water temperatures are high, large concentrations of Atlantic croaker are found inside 20 m, often in bays and estuaries. When inshore bottom temperatures drop during late fall and winter, the fish move offshore, and the largest concentrations are found between 20 and 60 m.

    Spawning occurs offshore in fall and winter, and eggs and larvae are transported to estuaries that serve as nursery grounds. Atlantic croaker are commonly up to 12 inches long (total length), but can grow up to 24 inches, weigh up to 4 pounds, and typically live up to 4 years. Adults feed on bottom-dwelling fish and invertebrates. (Texas Parks & Wildlife Department, Florida Fish and Wildlife Conservation Commission, Louisiana Sea Grant)

    This map shows the fisheries-independent (federal and state) trawl stations used to calculate catch per unit effort (CPUE) data for Atlantic croaker.

    Fisheries-Independent Data
    Federal

    Using data from federal sources, maps displaying the relative abundance (number per hour) of Atlantic croaker and sampling effort by 10-minute longitude by latitude blocks are available. This information was obtained by summarizing 11,637 40-ft shrimp trawls taken during the Southeast Area Monitoring and Assessment Program (SEAMAP) fishery-independent Summer and Fall Shrimp/Groundfish Surveys from 1987 to 2009. Relative abundance is expressed in catch per unit effort (CPUE), which represents the number caught per 1-hour tow. Sampling effort represents the number of trawls. These are primarily adult fish but may represent some juveniles as well.

    Fisheries-Independent Data
    State
    Using data from state sources, maps displaying Atlantic croaker mean catch per unit effort (CPUE) based on individual sampling locations, as well as by water bodies are available. The information shows the summarized results from 4,285 trawls (from primarily 16-ft to 20-ft otter trawls) taken from 2000 to 2007 in estuarine and nearshore habitats. This trawl data set was obtained from the Comparative Assessment of Gulf Estuarine ecoSystems (CAGES) Relational Database, which is a compilation of estuarine fishery-independent data contributed by natural resource agencies of the Gulf States (Texas, Louisiana, Mississippi, Alabama, and Florida). There are some differences in each state's gear and sampling methodologies, and CPUE values between states may not be directly comparable. CPUE is expressed in number caught per hectare.
