• Essential Fish Habitats - Highly Migratory Species
    Atlantic Bluefin Tuna

    The Atlantic bluefin tuna (Thunnus thynnus) is regarded as one of the most prized species in the ocean. It's also one of the biggest, reaching average lengths of 6.5 ft (1.8 m), and weighing about 550 lb (249.7 kg). One fish can sell for tens of thousands of dollars. The bluefin tuna are at the top of the food chain, which gives them an important role in the ecosystem. (NOAA's Atlantic Bluefin Tuna Report)

    Atlantic bluefin tuna occur between 45°N to 0° and are managed as distinct western and eastern stocks separated by a management boundary at the 45°W meridian. The prevailing assumption is that mature western bluefin tuna follow an annual cycle of foraging in June through March off the eastern United States and Canadian coasts, followed by migration to the Gulf of Mexico to spawn in April, May, and June.

    Essential Fish Habitat has been defined for three life stages (spawning, eggs, and larvae; juveniles; and adults) in Amendment 1 to the 2006 Consolidated Atlantic Highly Migratory Species Fishery Management Plan. The Essential Fish Habitat designations are represented in the maps and text descriptions in Amendment 1. A Habitat Area of Particular Concern (HAPC) was also identified for bluefin tuna in the Gulf of Mexico in Amendment 1, which extends west of 85°W longitude and south of 29°N latitude. All of the Essential Fish Habitat stages and HAPC are represented in the Gulf of Mexico Data Atlas, the NOAA EFH Mapper, and in Amendment 1.

    The habitat requirements for larval success are not known, but larvae are collected within narrow ranges of temperature and salinity-approximately 26°C and 36 ppt. It is believed that the western Atlantic stock matures at age 9. Adults are opportunistic feeders, preying on a variety of schooling fish, cephalopods, and benthic invertebrates, including silver hake, Atlantic mackerel, Atlantic herring, krill, sandlance, and squid. Currently, the U.S. has determined that the western Atlantic bluefin tuna stock is overfished with overfishing occurring. Bluefin tuna were designated as an Endangered Species Act Species of Concern in May 2011.
