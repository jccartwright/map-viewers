• Estuarine Bathymetry

    An estuary is the thin zone along a coastline (such as bays, lagoons, sounds or sloughs) where freshwater systems and rivers meet and mix with a salty ocean and become brackish water. Estuaries are known by other names, such as interior bays, lagoons, and sounds. (estuaries.noaa.gov)

    Because estuaries are governed by tides in the same way as the ocean, estuarine bathymetry supplies the integral component of models that are necessary to understand or predict movement of oil and hazardous materials, temperature and salinity distributions, animal migration, and modeling of storm-surge and tsunami effects.

    The estuarine bathymetry presented on the map represents an interpolated data set from the most current depth-sounding information available at the time of publication (1998). Twenty of the estuaries in the Gulf of Mexico are among the nation's most significant estuaries.
