      abstract: 
          This regional Gulf of Mexico dataset includes the following from the Harris et al., 2014 global seafloor 
          geomorphic features map: base layers (shelf, slope, abyss, hadal layers), shelf classification (mountains, 
          hills, plains classes), abyssal classification (high, medium. low classes), and the escarpments, basins, 
          canyons, seamounts, bridges, sills, shelf valleys, rift valleys, ridges, spreading ridges, fans, rises, 
          terraces, trenches, and plateaus layers.
      purpose: 
          These map layers represent the seafloor geomorphic features and shelf and abyssal habitat classifications in 
          the Gulf of Mexico and north-western Caribbean. These maps are are regional subset extracted from the global 
          seafloor geomorphic features map (Harris et.al. 2014) based on interpretation of the SRTM30 plus v7 global 
          bathymetry model, The global seafloor geomorphic features map comprises 25 feature layers, and is intended 
          to support ocean management including feature inventories, spatial planning and biodiversity conservation.
      credit: 
          The global seafloor geomorphic features map has been produced through a collaboration between Geoscience 
          Australia, GRID-Arendal and Conservation International (www.bluehabitats.org).
          Reference: Harris, P.T., Macmillan-Lawler, M., Rupp, J. and Baker, E.K. 2014. Geomorphology of the oceans. 
          Marine Geology, 352: 4-24.
      credit: 
          The Gulf of Mexico regional subset was produced by NOAA's National Centers for Environmental Information 
          (NCEI) for use in the NOAA Gulf of Mexico Data Atlas (gulfatlas.noaa.gov)
