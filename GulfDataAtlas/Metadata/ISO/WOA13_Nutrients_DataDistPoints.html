      Abstract: 
          These maps depict the number of observations per grid node used to calculate climatologies of dissolved 
          inorganic nutrient (phosphate, nitrate, silicate) concentration in the Gulf of Mexico. ArcGIS Shapefiles 
          containing the annual number of observations 1 degree lat-long grid data from the NOAA National 
          Oceanographic Data Center Ocean Climate Laboratory's World Ocean Atlas 2013 (WOA13) were used to generate 
          these maps.
      Purpose: 
          To show the number and spatial distribution of observations used to calculate the concentration of dissolved 
          inorganic nutrients in the Gulf of Mexico large marine ecosystem.

      Supplemental Information: 
          World Ocean Atlas 2013 (WOA13) is a set of objectively analyzed (1 degree grid and 1/4 degree grid) 
          climatological fields of in situ temperature, salinity, dissolved oxygen, Apparent Oxygen Utilization, 
          percent oxygen saturation, phosphate, silicate, and nitrate at standard depth levels between the surface and 
          the ocean bottom to a maximum depth of 5500m for annual, seasonal, and monthly compositing periods for the 
          World Ocean. 1/4 degree fields are for temperature and salinity only. It also includes associated 
          statistical fields of observed oceanographic profile data interpolated to standard depth levels on 1/4 
          degree, 1 degree, and 5 degree grids. Temperature and salinity fields are available for six decades 
          (1955-1964, 1965-1974, 1975-1984, 1985-1994, 1995-2004, and 2005-2012) and an average of all decades 
          representing the period 1955-2012. Nutrient and oxygen fields are available using all quality controlled 
          data for the entire sampling period 1878-2012.
          Climatologies are defined as "mean oceanographic fields at selected standard depth levels based on the 
          objective analysis of historic oceanographic profiles and select surface-only data" (Garcia et al, 2014).
