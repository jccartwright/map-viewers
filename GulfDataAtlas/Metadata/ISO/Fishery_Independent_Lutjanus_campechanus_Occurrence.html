      Abstract: 
          Larval, juvenile and adult red snapper (Lutjanus campechanus) are routinely taken during Southeast Area 
          Monitoring and Assessment Program (SEAMAP) and National Marine Fisheries Service (NMFS) plankton, trawl, 
          reeffish and bottom longline fishery-independent resource surveys throughout the U.S. Gulf of Mexico. The 
          SEAMAP Fall Plankton Survey covers the U.S continental shelf from Brownsville, TX to Key West, Florida 
          during the spawning season of red snapper. Juvenile red snapper are caught during SEAMAP Summer and Fall 
          Shrimp/Groundfish trawl surveys. The Summer and Fall surveys cover the U.S. continental shelf from 
          Brownsville, TX to Mobile, AL in depths ranging from 9 to 110 meters. In the fall of 2008, the surveys were 
          expanded to include the West Florida shelf. Adult red snapper are primary observed/taken during SEAMAP 
          Reeffish and NMFS Bottom Longline surveys. The Reef Fish Survey samples species associated with topographic 
          features (natural reefs, banks and ledges) located on the continental shelf of the Gulf of Mexico in from 
          Brownsville, Texas to the Dry Tortugas, FL. The Bottom Longline survey covers the continental shelf from 
          Brownsville, TX to the Dry Tortugas in depths ranging from 9 to 360 meters. These surveys are used to map 
          the occurrence (presence or absence) of red snapper at sampling stations throughout the U.S. Gulf of Mexico. 
          Larval occurrence is based on 61 centimeter bongo net samples collected during Fall Plankton Surveys from 
          1986 to 2006. Juvenile occurrence is based on samples collected by 40 foot shrimp trawls during Summer and 
          Fall Shrimp/Groundfish Surveys from 1987 to 2009. Adult occurrence is based on video camera observations 
          during Reeffish Surveys between 1993 and 2009, and fish collected during Bottom Longline Surveys from 2000 
          to 2009.
      Purpose: 
          The occurrence (prescence or absence) of red snapper (Lutjanus campechanus) are mapped from 
          fishery-independent SEAMAP and NMFS resouce surveys in the Gulf of Mexico.

      Supplemental Information: 
          SEAMAP is a State/Federal/university program for collection, management and dissemination of 
          fishery-independent data and information in the southeastern United States. Resource surveys began in the 
          Gulf of Mexico in 1982. These surveys currently include Winter, Summer, and Fall Shrimp/Groundfish Surveys; 
          Winter, Spring, and Fall Plankton Surveys; a Reeffish Survey; a Vertical Longline Survey; and an Inshore 
          Bottom Longline Survey. These surveys collect a variety of biological and environmental information. All 
          SEAMAP data are available upon request. For information concerning SEAMAP, please contact Jeff Rester of the 
          Gulf States Marine Fisheries Commission at (228) 875-5912 or via e-mail at jrester@gsmfc.org. The National 
          Marine Fisheries Service (NMFS), Southeast Fisheries Science Center (SEFSC), Mississippi Laboratories, 
          Resource Survey Division conducts fishery-independent resource surveys throughout the southeast region of 
          the United States. The primary goal of these surveys is to monitor the abundance, distribution, and health 
          of living marine resources and their habitats. Current operations include groundfish, longline, marine 
          mammal, plankton and reef fish surveys. For information concerning the Mississippi Laboratories surveys, 
          please contact Dr. Terry Henwood, Branch Chief Resource Surveys at (228) 762-4591 or via e-mail at 
          terry.henwood@noaa.gov.
