      Supplemental Information: 
          The NODC Gulf of Mexico Regional Climatology is a set of objectively analyzed mean climatological fields (1 
          degree grid, 1/4 degree grid, and 1/10 degree grid) of in situ temperature and salinity at 102 standard 
          depth levels between the surface and the ocean bottom to a maximum depth of 5500m for annual, seasonal, and 
          monthly compositing periods for the Gulf of Mexico. Climatologies are defined as "mean oceanographic fields 
          at selected standard depth levels based on the objective analysis of historic oceanographic profiles and 
          select surface-only data" (Garcia et al, 2014).
