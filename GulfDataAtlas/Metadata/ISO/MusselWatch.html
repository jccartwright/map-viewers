      Abstract: 
          The MWANALYTE data file reports the trace concentrations of a suite of chemical contaminants in marine 
          sediment and bivalve tissue samples collected from all U.S. coastal regions since 1986. The sediment and 
          tissue samples were analyzed for major and trace elements and suite of organic chemical constituents, 
          including butlytins, polynuclear aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs), polychlorinated biphenyls (PCBs), and 
          pesticides. The MWANALYTE file is constructed as a vertical formatted table.
      Purpose: 
          The main objectives of the NS&T Program are the following: (1) Conduct long-term monitoring of contaminants 
          and other environmental conditions at more than 300 sites along all U.S. coasts, (2) Advise and participate 
          in local, regional, national, and international projects related to coastal monitoring and assessment. The 
          objective of the sediment and tissue chemistry data file is to report the concentrations of chemical 
          contaminants measured in NS&T marine samples. Detailed detection limits can be found in: Lauenstein, G. G. 
          and A. Y. Cantillo (1998) Analytical Methods of the National Status and Trends Program Mussel Watch Project 
          - 1993 -1997 Update. NOAA Technical Memorandum NOS ORCA 130, Silver Spring, MD. Lauenstein, G. G. and A. Y. 
          Cantillo (1993) Sampling and analytical methods of the National Status and Trends Program National Benthic 
          Surveillance and Mussel Watch Projects 1984-1992: Overview and summary of methods, Volume I NOAA Technical 
          Memorandum NOS ORCA 71, Silver Spring, MD.

      Report: 
        Completeness omission: 
          Evaluation method description: 
              Care was taken to minimize disturbance to the sediment grabs. Grab samples that were incomplete, slumped, 
              less than 7 cm in depth, or comprised chiefly of shelly substrates were discarded. The chance of 
              sampling the same location was minimized by repositioning the boat (five meters downstream) after three 
              sampling attempts. Bivalve collection criteria established for the MWP are: o Indigenous populations of 
              mollusks must exist at a potential sampling site since the monitoring effort does not use caged mussels. 
              o The NS&T bivalve sites coincide with historical monitoring sites (i.e., EPA Mussel Watch and 
              California Mussel Watch) when feasible and when all other criteria are met. o The site contains

                  composite samples from three stations. When this division of the site into stations was not 
                  possible, a pool of bivalves representative of the site was collected and divided into 3 separate 
                  collections. Each collection was treated as if it were from a station. This latter procedure was

                  CHEMICAL ANALYSIS Because analytical methods change with time, methods for a particular timeframe 
                  should be checked in the appropriate methods documents. Sediment and bivalve tissues samples were 
                  analyzed for metals, butyltins, PAHs, PCBs, pesticides and periodically additional analytes. The 
                  concentrations of chemical contaminants were measured in each sample. This information is useful 
                  both in characterizing the degree of contamination at a site, as well as providing information that 
                  may indicate the origin of the contaminants. (1) Total sediment metals were analyzed by completely 
                  digesting the samples in an aggressive acid treatment, thereby releasing all metals for measurement, 
                  including metals comprising the sediment sample particles themselves. This method analyzes both the 
                  trace metals which are most toxic to estuarine organisms and the relatively non-toxic metals of 
                  crustal origin such as aluminum, iron, and silicon which may be used to estimate the natural 
                  background levels of the toxic metals in sediments (see Strobel et al., 1995). (2) Metals quantified 
                  in bivalve tissues were also performed on samples that were totally dissolved. (3) Four butyltins 
                  were measured, reflecting interest in the distribution of the anti-fouling agent tributyltin and its 
                  breakdown products. (4) Three categories of organic contaminants were measured: PAHs, PCBs, and 
                  pesticides. Twenty-four core polynuclear aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs) were measured. An extended 
                  suite of alkylated PAHs was analyzed at selected sites in 1990-1992 and at all sites since 1993. 
                  Ancillary data such as temperature, salinity, sediment grain-size, Total Organic Carbon (TOC), and 
                  Total Inorganic Carbon (TIC) were also collected at each site. Sediment Chemistry: All analyses were 
                  performed on samples that were stored frozen. Samples analyzed for total metals were dried and 
                  completely digested in nitric/hydrofluoric/hydrochloric acids (acid persulfate for mercury). For the 
                  organic analyses, sediments were extracted using the procedures of NOAA National Status and Trends 
                  Program in Lauenstein and Cantillo (1993) and Lauenstein and Cantillo (1998). The analytical methods 
                  used were: (1) Cold vapor atomic absorption (AA) for mercury; (2) Flame AA for aluminum, iron, 
                  manganese, zinc; (3) Graphite furnace AA for arsenic, cadmium, chromium, copper, nickel, lead, 
                  selenium, silver, tin; (4) X-ray fluorescence for aluminum, arsenic, chromium, copper, iron, 
                  manganese, nickel, lead, silicone, zinc; (5) Neutron activation analysis for chromium, aluminum, 
                  iron, manganese; (6) Inductively coupled plasma atomic emission spectrometry (ICP-AES) for chromium, 
                  cadmium, nickel, lead, tin, antimony. (7) The PAHs were analyzed by gas chromatography / 
                  mass-spectrometry (GC/MS); pesticides and PCBs were analyzed by GC/ECD (electron capture detector); 
                  and the butyltins were analyzed by GC/FID (flame ionization detector). Concentrations are reported 
                  on a dry weight basis. Tissue Chemistry: All analyses were performed on samples that were stored 
                  frozen. Samples were dried and completely digested in nitric or in nitric and hydrochloric acids.
