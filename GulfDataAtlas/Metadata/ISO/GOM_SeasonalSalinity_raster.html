      Abstract: 
          These maps depict seasonal salinity climatologies at 10m and 250m depths in the Gulf of Mexico. Shapefiles 
          containing the objectively analyzed mean seasonal 1/10 degree lat-long grid data from the NOAA National 
          Oceanographic Data Center Ocean Climate Laboratory's Gulf of Mexico Regional Climatology were used to 
          generate these maps. For these maps, winter=January-March, spring=April-June, summer=July-September, 
          fall=October-December.
      Purpose: 
          To show the spatial distribution of salinity concentrations in the Gulf of Mexico large marine ecosystem.

      Supplemental Information: 
          The NODC Gulf of Mexico Regional Climatology is a set of objectively analyzed mean climatological fields (1 
          degree grid, 1/4 degree grid, and 1/10 degree grid) of in situ temperature and salinity at 102 standard 
          depth levels between the surface and the ocean bottom to a maximum depth of 5500m for annual, seasonal, and 
          monthly compositing periods for the Gulf of Mexico. Climatologies are defined as "mean oceanographic fields 
          at selected standard depth levels based on the objective analysis of historic oceanographic profiles and 
          select surface-only data" (Garcia et al, 2014).

              Description: 
                  ArcGIS point shapefiles for the seasonal objectively analyzed mean 1/10-degree grid for salinity were 
                  downloaded from the NOAA NODC Gulf of Mexico Regional Climatology data access site 
                  (http://www.nodc.noaa.gov/OC5/regional_climate/GOMclimatology/index.html). The shapefiles were 
                  loaded into ArcMap 10.2 and data points within the Gulf of Mexico were extracted using the Clip tool 
                  and saved as a separate shapefile. The Gulf of Mexico data points were then interpolated using the 
                  Spline with Barriers tool to create a raster image. Contours were created from the raster using the 
                  Contour tool and smoothed using the Smooth Line tool, then overlayed onto the raster.
