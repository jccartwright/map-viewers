      Abstract: 
          This hurricane dataset is a 10 minute grid based on a subset of the 1851 - 2012 hurricane tracks from the 
          International Best Track Archive for Climate Stewardship (IBTrACS) and the National Hurricane Center. The 
          grid has attributes added that allow analysis of hurricane frequency in the Gulf of Mexico. The grid only 
          references systems that were Tropical Storm strength or greater. Only tracks that entered the Gulf of Mexico 
          are included. (For example, storms that moved westward across Florida, but did not emerge into the Gulf, 
          were excluded.)
      Purpose: 
          This dataset was developed to include in the Gulf of Mexico Data Atlas to show hurricane frequency.

      Supplemental Information: 
          The grid shape is based on the definition of the Gulf of Mexico boundaries by the International Hydrographic 
          Organization (IHO). The grid area covering the Mexico and Florida peninsulas only includes the western 
          halves since the eastern halves would not be considered part of the Gulf of Mexico. At the time of the 
          development of this dataset, 2012 storm tracks were not included in the IBTrACS source and were obtained 
          separately from the National Hurricane Center. The frequency and maximum intensity attributes are broken out 
          into 25-year increments, except the 2001-2012 grouping. The 1926-1969, 1970-1994, and 1995-2012 groupings 
          are based on suggested multi-decadal patterns in a NOAA publication 
          (http://www.magazine.noaa.gov/stories/mag184.htm).

              Description: 
                  A fishnet grid was created covering an area between -99 to -79 degrees longitude and 17 to 31 degrees 
                  latitude. The grid was spaced in 10 minute intervals. Grid cells that did not fall within the Gulf 
                  of Mexico boundary were removed. A copy of the Gulf of Mexico storm track layer was edited to remove 
                  segments that were less than Tropical Storm strength, and the remaining storm segments were merged 
                  into single track lines for each storm. The grid was then spatially joined with this storm track 
                  layer to get a count of tracks falling within each grid cell. Storm tracks that fell on a grid line 
                  or corner are counted in two or more grid cells. A copy of the Gulf of Mexico storm track layer with 
                  storms less than Tropical Storm intensity removed, but still maintaining individual segments, was 
                  spatially joined in a one-to-many join with the grid layer to provide a cross reference to generate 
                  attributes regarding intensity.
