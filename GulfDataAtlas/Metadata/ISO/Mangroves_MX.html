      Lineage: 
        Lineage: 
          Process step: 
            Process step: 
              Description: 
                  After applying radiometric and geometric corrections to the images, another process was applied to 
                  mask and eliminate areas, both oceanic and terrestrial, not associated with the mangroves coverage. 
                  Threshold values were used in the NDVI and Digital Elevation Models to mask the water, bare soil and 
                  vegetation zones that were not mangroves. Recent images with better coverage and fewer clouds were 
                  selected for classification. The ISODATA algorithm was used as the classification technique. The 
                  resulting spectral categories were classified into two categories: mangrove and 'other'. Once the 
                  thematic map with the two above categories was completed, we applied an unsupervised classification 
                  process, similar to the one above, but involving only the mangrove class. This step allowed the 
                  separation of subclasses with different spectral responses which can hypothetically be used to 
                  identify categories such as shrub and woodland. However, this level of analysis could not be applied 
                  to the entire country because of the lack of in situ data and therefore in this first instance, the 
                  map displays only the mangrove class. The map was changed from raster to vector and any polygons 
                  smaller than 10000 m2 were removed, which corresponds to the minimum viewable area at the 1:50000 
                  scale.
