      Abstract: 
          These maps depict seasonal climatologies of dissolved oxygen concentration at 10m and 250m depths in the Gulf 
          of Mexico. Shapefiles containing the objectively analyzed mean seasonal 1 degree lat-long grid data from the 
          NOAA National Oceanographic Data Center Ocean Climate Laboratory's World Ocean Atlas 2013 (WOA13) were used 
          to generate these maps. For these maps, winter=January-March, spring=April-June, summer=July-September, 
          fall=October-December.
      Purpose: 
          To show the spatial distribution and concentration of dissolved oxygen in the Gulf of Mexico large marine 
          ecosystem.

      Supplemental Information: 
          World Ocean Atlas 2013 (WOA13) is a set of objectively analyzed (1 degree grid and 1/4 degree grid) 
          climatological fields of in situ temperature, salinity, dissolved oxygen, Apparent Oxygen Utilization, 
          percent oxygen saturation, phosphate, silicate, and nitrate at standard depth levels between the surface and 
          the ocean bottom to a maximum depth of 5500m for annual, seasonal, and monthly compositing periods for the 
          World Ocean. 1/4 degree fields are for temperature and salinity only. It also includes associated 
          statistical fields of observed oceanographic profile data interpolated to standard depth levels on 1/4 
          degree, 1 degree, and 5 degree grids. Temperature and salinity fields are available for six decades 
          (1955-1964, 1965-1974, 1975-1984, 1985-1994, 1995-2004, and 2005-2012) and an average of all decades 
          representing the period 1955-2012. Nutrient and oxygen fields are available using all quality controlled 
          data for the entire sampling period 1878-2012.
          Climatologies are defined as "mean oceanographic fields at selected standard depth levels based on the 
          objective analysis of historic oceanographic profiles and select surface-only data" (Garcia et al, 2014).

              Description: 
                  The ArcGIS point shapefile for the seasonal objectively analyzed mean 1-degree grid for dissolved 
                  oxygen downloaded from the NOAA NODC WOA13 data access site 
                  (http://www.nodc.noaa.gov/OC5/woa13/woa13data.html). The shapefile was loaded into ArcMap 10.2 and 
                  data points within the Gulf of Mexico were extracted using the Clip tool and saved as a separate 
                  shapefile. The Gulf of Mexico data points were then interpolated using the Spline with Barriers tool 
                  to create a raster image. Contours at intervals appropriate for each nutrient dataset were then 
                  created from the raster using the Contour tool and overlayed onto the raster.
