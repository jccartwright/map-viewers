      Abstract: 
          This is a final classification. It is ready for distribution pending review by NOAA-CSC staff. This data set 
          is the pre-hurricane Katrina 2006-era classification of the US Gulf Coast, zone 46. This data set utilized 
          11 full or partial Landsat 5 Thematic Mapper scenes which were analyzed according to the Coastal Change 
          Analysis Program (C-CAP) protocol to determine land cover.
      Purpose: 
          To improve the understanding of coastal uplands and wetlands, and their linkages with the distribution, 
          abundance, and health of living marine resources.

          Process step: 
            Process step: 
              Description: 
                  This dataset was created by MDA Federal. This classification is based on 2006-era Landsat TM imagery 
                  for areas of 2001-2005 change. The study area is zone 46 of the Gulf Coast. The 2000-era C-CAP 
                  classification available for the same area was updated using 2006-era imagery. Change detection 
                  analysis was performed to create a layer showing areas of change between 2000 and 2005. The change 
                  area was then classified and overlaid on the 2000-era dataset.
                  Pre-processing steps: Each Landsat TM scene was geo-referenced by USGS (United States Geological 
                  Survey) EROS Data Center. Then MDA Federal staff verified the scenes for spatial accuracy to within 
                  2 pixels. The data was geo-referenced to Albers Conical Equal Area, with a spheroid of GRS 1980, and 
                  Datum of WGS84. The data units is in meters. Single scene, 2000- and 2005-date imagery was received 
                  by MDA Federal from USGS-EDC (EROS Data Center) for use in this classification procedure. 
                  At-satellite reflectance was performed on each scene and the tasseled cap transformation applied. 
                  When possible, leaf-off 2005 imagery was selected for use. All of the image data used was Landsat TM 
                  5 or 7. The first step of the process was to remove clouds/cloud shadows from the 2005 imagery. 
                  Cloud/cloud shadow areas were filled with imagery from another 2006-era scene to create cloud-free 
                  imagery for zone 46. The tasseled cap scenes were mosaicked to create a continuous zone 46 dataset 
                  which was used for classification. The individual scenes were used for change detection.
                  Change Detection: The next step was to determine the areas of change between 2000 and 2006-era data. 
                  The change detection algorithm used is the Cross Correlation Analysis process (CCA) developed at MDA 
                  Federal. This copyrighted procedure produced 2 Z-score files per scene of likelihood of change. 
                  These files were thresholded and mosaicked to create a binary change layer for that scene. In 
                  addition to the CCA process, a model was used to determine the differences between the 2000 and 2005 
                  tasseled-cap layers. This layer was used to check for change areas that were not completely 
                  extracted by the CCA process. All of the binary files were mosaicked to create a change layer for 
                  the entire study area. A focal majority was run on the change layer to fill in some clumps to make 
                  sure all of the change was accounted for. The change layer is a slight over-estimation of change to 
                  make sure to include as much change as possible.
                  Classification: The classification of the change areas was a mixture of automated and manual 
                  approaches. The change areas were removed from the 2000 classification. The areas with no change 
                  between 2000 and 2005 were used as training for a Classification and Regression tree (CART)analysis 
                  of the changed areas. Modelling and hand-editing were used to further refine the CART output and 
                  create a final classification. The classified change areas were overlaid on the 2000 C-CAP product 
                  to create a 2005 C-CAP classification. Attributes for this product are as follows:
                  0 Background 1 Unclassified (Cloud, Shadow, etc) 2 High Intensity Developed 3 Medium Intensity 
                  Developed 4 Low Intensity Developed 5 Open Space Developed 6 Cultivated Land 7 Pasture/Hay 8 
                  Grassland 9 Deciduous Forest 10 Evergreen Forest 11 Mixed Forest 12 Scrub/Shrub 13 Palustrine 
                  Forested Wetland 14 Palustrine Scrub/Shrub Wetland 15 Palustrine Emergent Wetland 16 Estuarine 
                  Forested Wetland 17 Estuarine Scrub/Shrub Wetland 18 Estuarine Emergent Wetland 19 Unconsolidated 
                  Shore 20 Bare Land 21 Water 22 Palustrine Aquatic Bed 23 Estuarine Aquatic Bed 24 Tundra 25 Snow/Ice
