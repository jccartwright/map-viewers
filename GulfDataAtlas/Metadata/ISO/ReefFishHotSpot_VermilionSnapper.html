      Abstract: 
          Getis-Ord Gi analysis of vermilion snapper (Rhomboplites aurorubens) distribution was conducted using 
          observations recorded by the NOAA Fisheries Service Galveston Laboratory's Gulf of Mexico Reef Fish Observer 
          Program from 2007 through 2013 (28,087 sets made during 731 trips). Average capture was summarized by 15 
          minute grid blocks. The hot spot, or Getis-Ord Gi statistic, is used to detect clusters of high or low 
          catch per unit effort (CPUE) values. In ArcGIS, the hot spot tool looks at the value of each feature in 
          relation to the values for its neighboring features. A statistically significant Z-score results if the sum 
          for a feature and its neighbors, when compared proportionally to the sum of all features, is different from 
          what is expected. If a feature is surrounded by neighbors with similarly high (or low) values, then it is 
          part of a statistically significant hot (or cold) spot. Blocks with data from less than 3 vessels were 
          removed for confidentiality purposes.
      Purpose: 
          Getis-Ord Gi analysis of kept and discarded vermilion snapper, Rhomboplites aurorubens, capture from 
          observations recorded by NOAA Fisheries Service Galveston Laboratory Gulf of Mexico Reef Fish Observer 
          Program 2007-2013 (28,087 sets made during 731 trips). Average capture summarized by 15-minute grid blocks. 
          Blocks with less than 3 vessels were included in analysis, but removed before distribution for 
          confidentiality purposes.

              Description: 
                  Data Aggregation and Confidentiality: For confidentiality purposes, data were aggregated into 
                  15-minute longitude by latitude blocks and the average catch per unit effort (CPUE) per block was 
                  calculated. The authority to place observers on U.S. fishing vessels falls under the 
                  Magnuson-Stevens Fishery Conservation and Management Act (S. 2012--109th Congress, 2005). The act 
                  also requires that NMFS maintains the confidentiality of any information obtained through compliance 
                  with it. The National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) Administrative Rule 216-100 
                  (NOAA, 1994) outlines the protocols for maintaining confidentiality, while the NMFS Office of 
                  Science and Technology outlined techniques for aggregating and summarizing data. Any spatial or 
                  temporal data that is reported must contain at least three participants in the strata to protect the 
                  identity of the persons, businesses, fishing locations, time, gear, landings, or other information 
                  relevant to the commercial fishery. After computing the Getis-Ord Gi*, or hot spot, statistic, if 
                  any cells with less than 3 sets existed, they were removed to protect confidentiality, while not 
                  adversely affecting the results of the analysis.

          Process step: 
            Process step: 
              Description: 
                  Hot Spot Analysis: The hot spot, or Getis-Ord Gi* statistic, was used to detect clusters of high or 
                  low catch CPUE values. In ArcGIS, the hot spot tool looks at the value of each block in relation 
                  to the values for its neighboring blocks. A statistically significant Z-score results if the sum for 
                  a block and its neighbors, when compared proportionally to the sum of all blocks in the grid, is 
                  different from what is expected. If a feature is surrounded by neighbors with similarly high (or 
                  low) values, then it is part of a statistically significant hot (or cold) spot. Data in the map are 
                  symbolized by the resultant Z-scores. High Z-scores indicate a concentration of blocks with high 
                  CPUE values, while low (below 0) Z-scores indicate a concentration of low CPUE values. Z-scores near 
                  0 indicate no concentration of high or low values, i.e. blocks within the defined neighborhood have 
                  dissimilar values or are near the mean for the entire grid (ESRI ®, 2012). Z-scores are a measure of 
                  standard deviation. With a 95% confidence level, or p-value of 0.05, the critical Z-score values are 
                  -1.96 and +1.96 standard deviations; and -2.58 and +2.58 for a 99% confidence level, or p-value of 
                  0.01. A fixed distance band of 25 km, representative of the maximum length of a longline fishing 
                  set, was used to conceptualize the spatial relationships.
