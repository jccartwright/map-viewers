Title:
Occurrence of Vermillion Snapper (Rhomboplites aurorubens) from SEAMAP and NMFS Fishery-Independent Resource Surveys
Geospatial_Data_Presentation_Form:

Description:
Abstract:
Larval and juvenile vermilion snapper (Rhomboplites aurorubens) are routinely taken during Southeast Area Monitoring and Assessment Program (SEAMAP) plankton and trawl fishery-independent resource surveys throughout the U.S. Gulf of Mexico. The SEAMAP Fall Plankton Survey covers the U.S continental shelf from Brownsville, TX to Key West, Florida during the spawning season of vermilion snapper. Juvenile vermilion snapper are caught during SEAMAP Summer and Fall Shrimp/Groundfish trawl surveys. The Summer and Fall surveys cover the U.S. continental shelf from Brownsville, TX to Mobile, AL in depths ranging from 9 to 110 meters. In the fall of 2008, the surveys were expanded to include the West Florida shelf. Larval occurrence is based on 61 centimeter bongo net samples collected during Fall Plankton Surveys from 1986 to 2006. Juvenile occurrence is based on samples collected by 40 foot shrimp trawls during Summer and Fall Shrimp/Groundfish Surveys from 1987 to 2009.
Purpose:
Fishery-independent trawl and plankton data from Southeast Area Monitoring and Assessment Program (SEAMAP) Summer and Fall Shrimp/Groundfish Surveys (1987 - 2009) and the Fall Plankton Survey (1986 - 2006) are used to map the occurrence of vermilion snapper. Stations from the Shrimp/Groundfish and Fall Plankton Surveys show whether vermilion snapper were caught at that particular station or not.
