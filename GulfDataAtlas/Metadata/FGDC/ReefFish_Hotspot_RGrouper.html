Description:
Abstract:
Getis-Ord Gi* analysis of red grouper (Epinephelus morio) distribution was conducted using observations recorded by the NOAA Fisheries Service Galveston Laboratory's Gulf of Mexico Reef Fish Observer Program from 2007 through 2011 (4,656 sets made during 176 trips). Average capture was summarized by 5 minute grid blocks. The ‘hot spot’, or Getis-Ord Gi* statistic, is used to detect clusters of high or low catch per unit effort (CPUE) values. In ArcGIS™, the ‘hot spot’ tool looks at the value of each feature in relation to the values for its neighboring features. A statistically significant Z-score results if the sum for a feature and its neighbors, when compared proportionally to the sum of all features, is different from what is expected. If a feature is surrounded by neighbors with similarly high (or low) values, then it is part of a statistically significant hot (or cold) spot. Blocks with less than 3 sets were removed for confidentiality purposes.
Purpose:
The purpose of these vector digital data are to illustrate the spatial distribution and relative concentrations of red grouper based on fishery-dependent data in the Gulf of Mexico. This spatial data product is intended for display in the Gulf of Mexico Data Atlas, and is derived from aggregated catch data recorded by the Gulf of Mexico Reef Fish Observer Program.

Process_Step:
Process_Description:
Observer Data Collection: The Gulf of Mexico (GOM) reef fishery consists of approximately 821 federally permitted vessels (SERO). Primary gears used include bottom longline and vertical line (bandit or handline). Amendment 22 to the Gulf of Mexico Fishery Management Council’s (GMFMC) Reef Fish Fishery Management Plan (GMFMC) dictates mandatory observer coverage. In July 2006, in collaboration with the commercial fishing industry and the GMFMC, the National Marine Fisheries Service’s (NMFS) Southeast Fisheries Science Center (SEFSC) implemented a mandatory observer program to characterize the commercial reef fishery operating in the U.S. Gulf of Mexico. Characteristics of this year-round fishery and sampling protocol for the GOM reef fish observer program are described by Scott-Denton et al. (2011). The dataset used in this analysis is based on observer coverage of the U.S. Gulf of Mexico bottom longline fishery from 2007 through 2011 (4,656 sets made during 176 trips).

Process_Step:
Process_Description:
Data Aggregation and Confidentiality: For confidentiality purposes, data were aggregated into 5-minute longitude by latitude blocks and the average catch per unit effort (CPUE) per block was calculated. The authority to place observers on U.S. fishing vessels falls under the Magnuson-Stevens Fishery Conservation and Management Act (S. 2012--109th Congress, 2005). The act also requires that NMFS maintains the confidentiality of any information obtained through compliance with it. The National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) Administrative Rule 216-100 (NOAA, 1994) outlines the protocols for maintaining confidentiality, while the NMFS Office of Science and Technology outlined techniques for aggregating and summarizing data. Any spatial or temporal data that is reported must contain at least three participants in the strata to protect the identity of the persons, businesses, fishing locations, time, gear, landings, or other information relevant to the commercial fishery. After computing the Getis-Ord Gi*, or ‘hot spot’, statistic, if any cells with less than 3 sets existed, they were removed to protect confidentiality, while not adversely affecting the results of the analysis.

Process_Step:
Process_Description:
Hot Spot Analysis: The ‘hot spot’, or Getis-Ord Gi* statistic, is used to detect clusters of high or low catch CPUE values. In ArcGIS™, the ‘hot spot’ tool looks at the value of each block in relation to the values for its neighboring blocks. A statistically significant Z-score results if the sum for a block and its neighbors, when compared proportionally to the sum of all blocks in the grid, is different from what is expected. If a feature is surrounded by neighbors with similarly high (or low) values, then it is part of a statistically significant hot (or cold) spot. Data in the map are symbolized by the resultant Z-scores. High Z-scores indicate a concentration of blocks with high CPUE values, while low (below 0) Z-scores indicate a concentration of low CPUE values. Z-scores near 0 indicate no concentration of high or low values, i.e. blocks within the defined neighborhood have dissimilar values or are near the mean for the entire grid (ESRI ®, 2012). Z-scores are a measure of standard deviation. With a 95% confidence level, or p-value of 0.05, the critical Z-score values are -1.96 and +1.96 standard deviations; and -2.58 and +2.58 for a 99% confidence level, or p-value of 0.01. A fixed distance band of 25 km, representative of the maximum length of a longline fishing set, was used to conceptualize the spatial relationships.

Entity_Type_Definition:
Vector digital data (shapefile) in 5-minute blocks showing aggregated catch data for all red grouper recorded by fishery observers within each block, including both retained and discarded grouper. Catch data for each block include Getis-Ord Gi statistic Z-scores, Gi statistic p-value, set count, mean catch-per-unit-effort (CPUE), minimum and maximum CPUE, and CPUE standard deviation.
