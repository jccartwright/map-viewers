Description:
Abstract:
Projected land cover land use (LCLU) coverage for 2030 was generated using the Prescott Spatial Growth Model. The 2001 LCLU data was used as an existing baseline. Model assumptions included a continuation of existing population and spatial growth patterns. Growth rules allowed developed land areas to be attracted to coastal areas. Wetlands were preserved from development. The year 2030 LCLU model projection provides results for the same classification system used to develop the 1948, 1992, and 2001 land cover land use coverages.
Purpose:
The projected 2030 digital LCLU coverage for watersheds in Mobile and Baldwin Counties was developed for use as an input in a hydrologic modeling system to determine the effects of land cover land used change on shallow water aquatic ecosystems.
Supplemental_Information:
The 2030 model projections was used as input to the hydrologic modeling system.

Process_Step:
Process_Description:
The 2001 Land Use Land Cover coverage was used as the existing land cover land use baseline for the Prescott Spatial Growth Model (PSGM). The purpose of the PSG model was to generate a projection for LCLU 2030. Assumptions (in the model) included a continuation of existing population and spatial growth patterns. Growth was attracted to coastal areas and wetlands were protected in the model simulation. The Prescott Spatial Growth Model (PSGM) is an Arc geographic information system (GIS) compatible application that allocates future growth into available land based on user-defined parameters. Inputs to the model are current land use, current projected population, employment, and road networks. Methodology used in the PSGM is described in a similar study by the authors: Estes, Jr., M. G., M. Al-Hamdan, W. Crosson, D. A. Quattrochi, H. Johnson III, and J. Hodgson; “Validation and Demonstration of the Prescott Spatial Growth Model in Metropolitan Atlanta, Georgia”, Urban and Regional Information Systems Journal, Vol. 22, No. 1, pp. 5-21. August, 2010, found online here: http://www.urisa.org/Volume22No2.

Entity_and_Attribute_Information:
Overview_Description:
Entity_and_Attribute_Overview:
This projected 2030 land cover land use (LCLU) raster dataset used in hydrologic modeling contains the same eleven (11) remapped classes as the 1992 and 2001 NLCD data: water, low intensity/Recreational, medium/high density residential, urban commercial, bare soil/transitional, deciduous forest, evergreen forest, mixed forest/shrub, agricultural/pasture, woody wetlands, and emergent herbaceous wetlands.
