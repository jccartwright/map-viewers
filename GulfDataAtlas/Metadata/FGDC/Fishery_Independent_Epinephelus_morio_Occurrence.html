Description:
Abstract:
Red grouper (Epinephelus morio) are caught during Southeast Area Monitoring and Assessment Program (SEAMAP) and National Marine Fisheries Service (NMFS) reeffish and bottom longline fishery-independent resource surveys throughout the U.S. Gulf of Mexico. The SEAMAP Reef Fish Survey samples species associated with topographic features (natural reefs, banks and ledges) located on the continental shelf of the Gulf of Mexico in from Brownsville, Texas to the Dry Tortugas, FL. The NMFS Bottom Longline Survey covers the continental shelf from Brownsville, TX to the Dry Tortugas in depths ranging from 9 to 360 meters. These surveys are used to map the occurrence (presence or absence) of red grouper at sampling stations throughout the U.S. Gulf of Mexico. Occurrence is based on video camera observations during Reeffish Surveys between 1993 and 2009, and fish collected during Bottom Longline Surveys from 2000 to 2009.
Purpose:
The occurrence (prescence or absence) of red grouper (Epinephelus morio) are mapped from fishery-independent SEAMAP and NMFS resouce surveys in the Gulf of Mexico.
Supplemental_Information:
SEAMAP is a State/Federal/university program for collection, management and dissemination of fishery-independent data and information in the southeastern United States. Resource surveys began in the Gulf of Mexico in 1982. These surveys currently include Winter, Summer, and Fall Shrimp/Groundfish Surveys; Winter, Spring, and Fall Plankton Surveys; a Reeffish Survey; a Vertical Longline Survey; and an Inshore Bottom Longline Survey. These surveys collect a variety of biological and environmental information. All SEAMAP data are available upon request. For information concerning SEAMAP, please contact Jeff Rester of the Gulf States Marine Fisheries Commission at (228) 875-5912 or via e-mail at jrester@gsmfc.org. The National Marine Fisheries Service (NMFS), Southeast Fisheries Science Center (SEFSC), Mississippi Laboratories, Resource Survey Division conducts fishery-independent resource surveys throughout the southeast region of the United States. The primary goal of these surveys is to monitor the abundance, distribution, and health of living marine resources and their habitats. Current operations include groundfish, longline, marine mammal, plankton and reef fish surveys. For information concerning the Mississippi Laboratories surveys, please contact Dr. Terry Henwood, Branch Chief Resource Surveys at (228) 762-4591 or via e-mail at terry.henwood@noaa.gov.
