Description:
Abstract:
This dataset outlines areas in the Gulf of Mexico where spatial food webs have been identified based on king mackerel (Scomberomorus cavalla) diet data. These data were taken from a collection of 27 existing gut-content analysis studies comprising 1454 predator-prey interactions at the species level (available via the GoMexSI Gulf of Mexico Species Interactions online database http://gomexsi.tamucc.edu/). In this dataset, these interactions are summarized to the family level where possible. Prey items unable to be identified at the family level taxon are grouped in Class Actinopterigii. The percent volume of each prey family in each of the 6 food webs are listed along with the number of prey stomachs examined in each food web.
Purpose:
To identify the predator-prey interactions of king mackerel and to construct spatial food webs based on it's primary habitat and prey availablility.

Process_Step:
Process_Description:
King mackerel gut content data for the Gulf of Mexico were compiled from 27 existing sources comprising 1454 samples. These references and data can be browsed and accessed on the GoMexSI database website (http://gomexsi.tamu.edu). The data were divided into six food webs based on spatial occurrence, and aggregated under the appropriate family taxon. Prey items unable to be identified at the family level taxon are grouped in Class Actinopterigii. The percent volume of each prey family in each of the 6 food webs are listed, along with the number of prey stomachs examined in each food web.

Entity_and_Attribute_Information:
Overview_Description:
Entity_and_Attribute_Overview:
This shapefile contains polygon features defining the boundaries of six food webs for king mackerel in the Gulf of Mexico. The attributes for these features are the food web number, the percent volume occurrence of 15 prey families and the number of predator stomachs examined per food web. The family abbreviations are as follows: PEN- Penaeidae, TEU- Teuthidae, ENG- Engraulidae, CLU- Clupaeidae, MUG- Mugilidae, EXO- Exocetidae, HEM- Hemiramphidae, SER- Serranidae, CAR- Carangidae, LUT- Lutjanidae, SCI- Sciaenidae, MUL- Mullidae, SCA- Scaridae, TRI- Trichuridae, SCO- Scombridae.
