Description:
Abstract:
These maps depict seasonal temperature climatologies at 10m and 250m depths in the Gulf of Mexico. Shapefiles containing the objectively analyzed mean seasonal 1/10 degree lat-long grid data from the NOAA National Oceanographic Data Center Ocean Climate Laboratory's Gulf of Mexico Regional Climatology were used to generate these maps. For these maps, winter=January-March, spring=April-June, summer=July-September, fall=October-December.
Purpose:
To show the spatial distribution of seawater temperatures in the Gulf of Mexico large marine ecosystem.
Supplemental_Information:
The NODC Gulf of Mexico Regional Climatology is a set of objectively analyzed mean climatological fields (1 degree grid, 1/4 degree grid, and 1/10 degree grid) of in situ temperature and salinity at 102 standard depth levels between the surface and the ocean bottom to a maximum depth of 5500m for annual, seasonal, and monthly compositing periods for the Gulf of Mexico. Climatologies are defined as "mean oceanographic fields at selected standard depth levels based on the objective analysis of historic oceanographic profiles and select surface-only data" (Garcia et al, 2014).

Process_Step:
Process_Description:
ArcGIS point shapefiles for the seasonal objectively analyzed mean 1-degree grid for temperature were downloaded from the NOAA NODC Gulf of Mexico Regional Climatology data access site (http://www.nodc.noaa.gov/OC5/regional_climate/GOMclimatology/index.html). The shapefiles were loaded into ArcMap 10.2 and data points within the Gulf of Mexico were extracted using the Clip tool and saved as a separate shapefile. The Gulf of Mexico data points were then interpolated using the Spline with Barriers tool to create a raster image. Contours were created from the raster using the Contour tool and smoothed using the Smooth Line tool, then overlayed onto the raster.

Overview_Description:
Entity_and_Attribute_Overview:
These raster layers show seawater temperature in degrees Celcius. The values in each cell are interpolated from a 1/10 degree grid of statistically analysed measurements taken at the surface and throughout the water column.
