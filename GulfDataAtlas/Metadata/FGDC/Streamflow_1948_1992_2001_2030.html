Description:
Abstract:
The watershed model Loading Simulation Program in C++ (LSPC) was run for 22 subwatersheds adjacent to Mobile Bay to simulate streamflow for different Land Cover Land Use (LCLU) scenarios (1948, 1992, 2001, and 2030). Statistics of daily streamflow differences were developed comparing differences in 1992, 2001, and 2030 to the 1948 baseline.
Purpose:
Evaluate the impact of Land Cover Land Use (LCLU) change on streamflow.

Process_Step:
Process_Description:
Watershed modeling was completed to understand the impact of the projected urban development activities on the hydrologic system discharging into the Mobile Bay estuary. The Loading Simulation Program in C++ (LSPC) model (US EPA) was used for simulating hydrology and sediments at the watershed scale. LCLU scenarios were developed for 1992 and 2001 from the NLCD and in 2030 using the Prescott Spatial Growth Model (PSGM) to evaluate impacts of flows into Mobile Bay from adjacent sub-watershed basins. A map of 1948 land cover was used to represent a “green” pre-development scenario. All variables except LCLU were held constant for each model run. The LSPC model output provides changes in flow, temperature, sediments and general water quality for the 22 discharge points into the Bay

Entity_and_Attribute_Information:
Overview_Description:
Entity_and_Attribute_Overview:
Watershed model outputs for each LULC scenarios (1948, 1992, 2001, and 2030)are provided for each of the 22 contiguous subwatersheds that discharge into Mobile Bay and parts of Mississippi Sound and Wolf Bay. Streamflow differences between the baseline historical year of 1948 and the other LULC scenarios and sediment loads per subwatershed are provided.
