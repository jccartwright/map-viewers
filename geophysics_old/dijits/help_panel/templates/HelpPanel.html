Identifying surveys:

  • Single-click on the map to identify surveys (operates on visible layers).
  • : Identify surveys by drawing a rectangle on the map.
  • : Identify surveys by entering coordinates to define a bounding box.
  • A popup will appear with a list of the surveys near the click point or geographic area defined.
  • Mouse-over the list to highlight individual surveys on the map.
  • Click the magnifying glass icon to zoom to the extent of a survey.
  • Click on a row to view the attributes for that survey.
  • Click Show Summary to view a summary of all identified surveys.

Searching for surveys:

  • Search Marine Surveys or Search Airborne Surveys: opens a search dialog where a filter can be applied to the data shown on the map.
  • The current search criteria will be displayed in the Layers panel.
  • Reset Searches: resets the filter for both marine and airborne surveys.

Requesting data (3 different options):

  1. After identifying surveys, click Get Marine Data for These Surveys in the popup to open a new page where you can request and download the data for the currently-identified set of surveys (marine surveys only).
  2. Click Get Marine Data in the Layers panel to open a new page and request data that meet the search criteria, without defining a geographic area first (marine surveys only).
  3. After identifying surveys, click on a row to view the attributes. The Survey ID or Survey Link will link to a new page to request the data for that survey (marine or airborne).

Map navigation:

  • Click and drag the map to pan
  • SHIFT + Click to recenter
  • SHIFT + Drag to zoom in to a box
  • SHIFT + CTRL + Drag to zoom out
  • Mouse Scroll Forward to zoom in
  • Mouse Scroll Backward to zoom out
  • Use Arrow keys to pan
  • + key to zoom in a level
  • - key to zoom out a level
  • SHIFT + Double Click to Center and Zoom in
