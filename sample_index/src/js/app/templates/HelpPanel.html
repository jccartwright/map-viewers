Navigating the map
  • Click and drag or use arrow keys to pan
  • Mouse scroll forward or use + key to zoom in
  • Mouse scroll backward or use - key to zoom out
Identifying features
You have several options to identify geologic samples on the map:
  • Single-click on the map
  • Or, choose another tool from the "Identify" menu:
  • Click on to draw a rectangle
  • Click on to draw a polygon
  • Click on to enter coordinates for a bounding box
A popup will appear with a list of the selected samples. Mouse-over the list of samples to highlight them on the map (green circle). Click the magnifying glass icon to zoom to that feature. Click on a row to view attributes for that feature.

Search Samples: specify any combination of repository name, survey ID, platform name, lake name, range of years, device, and range of water depths. After clicking "Search Samples", a filter will be applied to the map layer, displaying only data of interest. The current number of points displayed on the map is shown below.
Reset: clears the current filter.

Show Table: Display a table displaying geologic sample information, with the current filter applied. The table can display a maximum of 50,000 samples. If the "Show Table" button is disabled, please apply a filter to display a smaller number of samples. Click on table rows (select multiple with shift or ctrl-click) to highlight samples on the map. Click on the column headers to sort on that field.
