What is the Index to Marine and Lacustrine Geological Samples?

The IMLGS is a tool to help scientists locate and obtain geologic material from sea floor and lakebed cores, grabs, and dredges archived by participating institutions. Before proposing research on any sample, please contact the Curator for sample condition and availability.

The IMLGS includes basic collection and storage information. Descriptions and additional information are available for some samples, and links to related data and images at NCEI and elsewhere. Alternative interfaces and web services are available.

Data Sources and Credits:

Data are prepared by the Curators. Some U.S. repositories receive support from the National Science Foundation (NSF). NCEI operates the IMLGS on behalf of the Curators' group, and is the NSF-designated National Data Center and archive for data and information about geologic samples collected with NSF funds.

  • Shaded Relief (GEBCO_2014): A shaded-relief visualization of the GEBCO_2014 Grid. This visualization of the GEBCO_2014 grid was produced by NOAA/NCEI. More information on NOAA's GeoPlatform.
  • Global Multi-Resolution Topography (GMRT) synthesis, Lamont-Doherty Earth Observatory, Columbia University. Two versions of the basemap are available: masked (highlights high-resolution multibeam swaths and contributed DEMs), and unmasked.
  • Shaded Relief (ETOPO1): A visualization of the ETOPO1 Global Relief Model. More information on NOAA's GeoPlatform.
  • Light Gray: Esri Light Gray Canvas
  • Arctic Basemap: A visualization of the IBCAO and GEBCO_08 grids. More information on NOAA's GeoPlatform.
  • Antarctic Basemap: A visualization of the IBCSO and GEBCO_08 grids. More information on NOAA's GeoPlatform.
  • Imagery: Esri World Imagery
  • Ocean: Esri Ocean Basemap
  • National Geographic: Esri/National Geographic Basemap
